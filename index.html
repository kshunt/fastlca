Methodology & data sources — click to expand ▼
FastLCA calculates GWP in kg CO₂e following the module structure of EN 15804+A2:2019. For user-entered EPD products: GWP-total (fossil + biogenic + LULUC per EN 15804+A2). For generic EPiC materials: Embodied Greenhouse Gas Emissions — a hybrid LCI aggregate equivalent to GWP-total but derived from a different methodology (process + input-output) and methodology of ISO 14040/14044. Results are indicative estimates — not a certified LCA or EPD.
⚠ Important — EPiC is an Australian database. Generic values use Australian input-output data and manufacturing conditions. The hybrid method (process + input-output) gives higher values than European EPDs because it captures indirect supply chain processes that process-only methods miss. For European/UK projects: use values as upper-bound estimates and replace with local EPDs (EPD Norway, IBU, Environdec) for formal reporting. Net recovery benefit. Always reported separately — never added to A1–C4 total per EN 15804+A2:2019 clause 6.4.3.4.

📋 EU regulatory context — why this matters now
🪪 Digital Product Passport (DPP) — mandatory for construction products 2027–2030 ▼

The Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR, Regulation EU 2024/1781) entered into force in July 2024. It mandates a Digital Product Passport for construction products — a machine-readable digital record that travels with the product throughout its life cycle, containing origin, material composition, carbon footprint, recycled content, durability, and end-of-life data.

The Construction Products Regulation (CPR, Regulation EU 2024/1305) specifically extends DPP requirements to construction materials. Together these regulations mean that placing a construction product on the EU market without a DPP will become illegal.

🚫 EU anti-greenwashing rules — already binding from September 2026 Binding Sep 2026 ▼

Two EU directives now restrict how companies can make environmental and climate claims — even in B2B contexts relevant to construction:

Empowering Consumers for the Green Transition Directive (EmpCo / ECGT, Directive EU 2024/825) — adopted March 2024, binding from 27 September 2026 across all EU member states. This is already law.

From September 2026 it will be illegal to:

  • Make generic environmental claims — "eco-friendly", "green", "sustainable", "natural", "circular" — without proof
  • Claim a product is "climate neutral" or "carbon neutral" based on offsetting rather than actual emission reductions
  • Display a sustainability label that hasn't been verified by a recognised certification scheme
  • Claim environmental benefit for only part of a product while implying it applies to the whole

Green Claims Directive (proposed) — would have added mandatory third-party pre-verification for all explicit environmental claims. The Commission announced intention to withdraw the proposal in June 2025 after political opposition to its bureaucratic burden on small businesses. The legislative process is paused — but not cancelled. The EmpCo Directive (above) still applies regardless.

💡 What this means for you: If your product will carry any carbon or environmental claim after September 2026 — on a website, in a tender, in a sales brochure — it needs to be substantiated with verifiable data. A verified EPD with declared GWP values from an accredited LCA programme is exactly the kind of evidence that makes a claim defensible. FastLCA estimates are not sufficient for public claims — they're a starting point.
