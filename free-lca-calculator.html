What FastLCA calculates

FastLCA performs a streamlined life cycle assessment focused on Global Warming Potential (GWP), expressed in kg CO₂e. It covers the full EN 15804+A2:2019 module structure:

  • A1–A3 Product stage — raw material extraction, transport to factory, and manufacturing. The primary focus of most early-stage LCA work.
  • A4–A5 Construction stage — transport to site and on-site construction energy and waste.
  • C1–C4 End of life — demolition, waste transport, processing, and disposal.
  • Module D — net benefit from recycling or reuse, always reported separately per EN 15804.

⚠ FastLCA provides indicative estimates for early-stage design decisions — not a certified LCA or EPD. For formal compliance reporting, engage a certified LCA practitioner.

Data sources

EPiC Database 2024 — generic materials

Generic material factors come from the Environmental Performance in Construction (EPiC) Database 2024, published by Crawford, Stephan and Prideaux at the University of Melbourne (DOI: 10.26188/10257728.v11). EPiC uses a hybrid life cycle inventory (LCI) approach combining process data and input-output data, which captures upstream indirect supply chain emissions that process-only EPDs miss. This makes EPiC values systematically higher than European manufacturer EPDs — not because EPiC is wrong, but because it's measuring more of the supply chain.

Licence: CC BY-NC-ND 4.0. Used as internal calculation input only.

EN 15804+A2:2019 — life cycle module structure

FastLCA follows the modular structure of EN 15804+A2:2019. Module D is always reported separately and never added to the A1–C4 total, per clause 6.4.3.4.

DESNZ 2025 — Scope 3 factors

The Scope 3 calculator uses UK Government DESNZ 2025 GHG conversion factors for fuel WTT, transport, waste disposal, and business travel. Open Government Licence v3.0.

Who is it for?

FastLCA is designed for sustainability professionals, architects, engineers, and contractors who need a quick, transparent embodied carbon estimate at early design stage — without the cost or complexity of certified LCA software. It is also useful for:

  • Students and academics learning LCA methodology
  • Manufacturers wanting to understand the embodied carbon of their products before commissioning a formal EPD
  • Sustainability consultants doing rapid screening before recommending formal LCA studies
  • Anyone comparing material options — is CLT or concrete lower carbon for this application?

Limitations

FastLCA is a screening tool, not a certified LCA platform. Key limitations:

  • EPiC values reflect Australian manufacturing conditions — use European EPDs for formal European project reporting
  • Only GWP is calculated — a full LCA includes acidification, eutrophication, resource use, and more
  • Use phase (B1–B7) is not included
  • Generic C1–C4 end-of-life factors are estimated, not from a single verified source
